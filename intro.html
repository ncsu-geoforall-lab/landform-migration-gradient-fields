Landscape Dynamics

Water, wind, gravitation, plants, animals and humans continuously change landscape surface Land surface processes are poorly understood and our predictive capabilities are limited Hazard Management decisions are often based on insufficient information

Advances in 3D mapping

Lidar and sUAS structure from motion dramatically increased our capacity to map 3D landscapes and assess the change on (almost) continuous basis Rapid development of tools for terrain time series analysis

Shoreline transects

We study evolving shorelines using transects. Transects divide shoreline and specify areas where we measure the changes. There are very good algorithms for creating transects, for example in GRASS GIS. However, transects for one set of shorelines might not work when shoreline changes significantly.

Connecting contours

Shorelines and contours are similar. To relate two different shorelines or contours we can draw the connections manually. This is a lot of work and it's impossible to reproduce.
